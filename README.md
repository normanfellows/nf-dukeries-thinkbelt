# nf-dukeries-thinkbelt

## Dukeries Thinkbelt

An ongoing investigation into distributed educational infrastructure, post-industrial territory, and anticipatory design in West Nottinghamshire.

---

## Introduction

Dukeries Thinkbelt (DTb) is a conceptual and territorial study based on the proposition that education, infrastructure, mobility, and civic life may be reorganised through distributed systems rather than centralised institutions.

Originally developed as a continuation and reinterpretation of Cedric Price’s *Potteries Thinkbelt* (1964–66), DTb explores the relationship between:

* learning,
* territory,
* post-industrial recovery,
* adaptive infrastructure,
* and alternative forms of social organisation.

The project is treated here not as a fixed historical design, but as an evolving anticipatory framework.

---

## Repository

This repository functions simultaneously as:

* archive,
* research environment,
* conceptual map,
* and working notebook.

It contains drawings, notes, references, territorial observations, and experimental structures that may continue to develop over time.

The repository is intentionally provisional.

---

## Core propositions

Current working propositions include:

* learning as a lifelong universal condition
* the network rather than the centralised campus
* mobility as educational infrastructure
* post-industrial territory as civic resource
* architecture as enabling framework rather than fixed enclosure
* education as a distributed social service
* research and learning as generators of settlement and community

These propositions remain open and incomplete.
